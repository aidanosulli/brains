# Vertical Jump Training: 25-Minute Protocol for Adding Inches Fast

## Core Principles

- **"Do the thing"** — to improve your vertical jump, prioritise actually jumping vertically; training must mirror the tested skill
- You cannot improve what you don't measure — establishing a baseline is non-negotiable before any training begins
- Progress is tracked through a simple loop: **measure → work → remeasure**
- This principle applies beyond basketball — to any measurable skill or goal

---

## Key Frameworks & Progressions

### Measurement Methods (choose one)
1. **Tape method** — stand ~1 ft from wall, mark standing reach with tape, jump and mark peak, measure the gap
2. **Ruler method** — same as above using a ruler if no tape measure available
3. **Backboard reference** — film yourself jumping to touch the backboard; no exact number, but a visual reference point for future comparison

### The 25-Minute Workout (3 progression stages)

| Stage | Activity | Duration |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Straight vertical jumps under the hoop — max height every rep, rest when tired | 5–10 min |
| 2 | Two-step approach jumps — isolate the final two steps (horizontal-to-vertical transfer); work both right-leg-forward and left-leg-forward | 5 min each side |
| 3 | Full approach jumps — tie it all together; work both left-right and right-left approaches | 5 min each side |

**Total: ~25–30 minutes**

### Review Cycle
- Train for **2 weeks**, then remeasure against original baseline
- Adjust based on results; repeat

---

## Actionable Advice

- Complete this workout **3–6 days per week** (Pat did 6 days/week and gained 5.3 inches in 30 days)
- On straight jumps and two-step jumps: **rest when tired** (1.5–2 min) — this is power/quality work, not endurance conditioning
- Work **both sides** on two-step and full approach jumps even if you're a one-foot jumper — asymmetry is common and training both sides closes the gap
- Set a timer for each stage to keep structure; don't rely on feel alone
- Leave the standing-reach tape on the wall as a permanent reference point for easy retesting

---

## Things to Avoid

- Starting training without a current vertical measurement — no baseline = no trackable progress
- Treating the jump sessions as cardio/endurance work — once form or height degrades, **stop and rest**
- Skipping the two-step isolation drill — this is where horizontal-to-vertical energy transfer happens and is the most overlooked component
- Training only your dominant approach side — always train both

---

## Key Takeaways

- **Measure first, always** — a specific starting number is the foundation of any improvement plan
- **Specificity wins** — the fastest way to jump higher is to spend dedicated time jumping as high as possible, not just lifting weights
- **The two-step drill is the key differentiator** — isolating the penultimate and final step targets the most critical moment of the jump
- **Volume and consistency beat complexity** — a simple 25–30 min protocol done 3–6x/week produced 5.3 inches of vertical gain in 30 days
- **Remeasure every 2 weeks** — data keeps you honest and reveals whether the program is working for your body
