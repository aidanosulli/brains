# Key Concepts: Another Vert Brain — 2026-07-07

## Jump Training Programming & Structure

- **Periodization**: Jump training organized across 3 sequential phases—Technique, Fundamentals, and Performance—to develop skills progressively.
- **Progressive Overload**: Each mesocycle builds on the previous one through sequenced progression of volume, intensity, strength, power, speed, and specificity.
- **Form Priority**: Proper exercise form takes priority over volume, with training stopping when form breaks or fatigue sets in.

## Training Load Management

- **Fatigue-Fitness Model**: Performance is a function of fitness minus fatigue, where macrocycles sequence training adaptations so fatigue dissipates and fitness peaks at the right time.
- **Ground Contact Management**: Total ground contacts per session are capped and progressively increased to manage training load and prevent excessive fatigue.

## Vertical Jump Types & Measurement

- **Standing Vertical Jump**: A type of vertical jump performed without an approach run or momentum phase.
- **Maximum Vertical Jump**: A type of vertical jump performed with an approach run or momentum phase to achieve maximum height.

## Performance Targets & Requirements

- **Elite Performance Target**: A 52.5-inch vertical jump represents an exceptionally elite, world-class level vertical jump measurement.
