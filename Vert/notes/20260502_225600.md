# Vertical Jump Training: 6-Mesocycle Blueprint (John Evans / THP Strength)

## Core Principles

- **Fatigue-Fitness Model:** Performance = fitness minus fatigue. Heavy training raises both simultaneously; you peak by strategically reducing fatigue while retaining fitness gains.
- **Progressive Overload:** Each mesocycle builds on the last — stacking adaptations sequentially to maximise genetic potential.
- **Specificity Spectrum:** General work (less jump-like) and specific work (more jump-like) must be inversely balanced. As specific work increases in intensity, general work must become *more* general to prevent injury and overload.
- **Potentiation Between Cycles:** Accessory work in each mesocycle primes the *following* mesocycle's main lifts — not just the current one.
- **Variety + Individuality + Rest:** Non-negotiable pillars alongside progressive overload.
- **Macrocycle Structure:** 6-month macrocycles, each containing 6 mesocycles (~1 month each), modelled on track & field periodisation.

---

## Key Frameworks & Progressions

### Macrocycle Overview (6 Mesocycles)

#### Month 1 — Volume Building (General Preparatory)
- **Reps:** 8–12 | **Sets:** 2–5 | **Intensity:** 65–70%
- Deload built into **Week 4**
- **Exercise type:** General work (less jump-specific)
  - Two-foot jumpers: sumo deadlift, unilateral work
  - One-foot jumpers: deep squats, full range of motion
- **Frequency:** 3 days/week (e.g. Mon/Wed/Fri lifting; Tue/Thu general days)
- **Session structure:**
  - Power exercise: ~4 reps × 5 sets
  - Squats: 4–5 sets × 6–12 reps
  - Accessories: 8–14 reps depending on fitness
- **General days (Tue/Thu):** High movement variety — "build an absolute unit"

#### Month 2 — Intensification (Specific Work Introduction)
- Move toward more **specific work** for two-foot jumpers
  - Clean pulls, power cleans, snatch pulls
  - Clean pulls / power cleans: 6 sets × 3–5 reps @ 90% (clean pulls) / 75–85% (power cleans)
- **Main lifts:** Drop to ~3 reps/set; total 5–7 sets (start low, add sets weekly)
- **Main lift rep range:** 6–8 (between hypertrophy and max strength)
- **Accessory work:** More specific — RDLs, calf raises at 4–6 reps @ up to 85%
- **General days:** Bodybuilding circuits (lower + upper body) + **tempo running** (30–45 sec efforts, ~3 min rest — aerobic/body composition focus, not lactic)

#### Month 3 — Absolute Strength
- **Squats:** 10 sets × 2–3 reps (e.g. 10×3 or 10×2 back squat)
- **Clean pulls:** 105–110% of power clean max (builds first-pull strength and second-pull force application)
- **Accessory work:** Eccentric strength — primer for next cycle
  - Nordic hamstring curls
  - Two-up/one-down calf raises
- **General days:** Jump circuits in **sand**, 20–30 seconds, varied jumps (toe touch, tuck, single-leg spinning hops); increased tempo running volume

#### Month 4 — Power / Strength-Speed
- **Power cleans:** Push to absolute maxes; 2×/week power clean + 1× clean pull day
- **Clean pulls at 105–110%** to make power cleans feel lighter
- **Eccentric overload (accentuated eccentrics):**
  - Belt squat: partner-assisted up phase; lower with 110–115% of max back squat
  - Eccentric split squats: 3 build-up sets normal + 3 working sets × 2–3 reps with supramaximal load (hold rack on concentric)
- **Partial ranges of motion** introduced:
  - One-foot jumpers: pull from blocks
  - Two-foot jumpers: full range from floor
- **Accessory work:** Elastic/velocity qualities — high-velocity calf raises, drop RDLs, bouncing calf raises (~5–6 reps × ~2 sets — cap here to avoid neural fatigue)
- **General days:** Pull back — bodyweight circuits, bodybuilding circuits; remove tempo runs and jump circuits (protect tendons/joints)

#### Month 5 — Bar Speed / Speed-Strength
- Transition from **strength-speed → speed-strength**
- **Olympic lifts:** Power snatch + lighter power cleans + hang power cleans
  - Power cleans: ~60% | Power snatch: ~50% of power clean max
  - Priority: maximal bar velocity
- **Squats:** Blend max strength + high-velocity
  - Primer: 5-4-3 ascending intensity to ~85%
  - High-velocity squats: 65–70% bar, timed set of 5 for max speed
- **Accessory work:** Shock loading primer for Month 6
  - Med ball / shot put throws (overhead, between legs, forward)
  - Low plyo volumes: box step-offs, step-up jumps — high variety, low volume
- **General days:** Maximally general — static flexibility, bodyweight circuits, core work; **no loaded leg work**; possible barefoot 100m walk-back × 10 reps; all jump circuits removed

#### Month 6 — Peak Specificity
- Two possible structures:
  - **Load management:** Jump Mon/Thu; "feel-good lifts" Tue/Fri (slow squats, slow calf raises, RDLs — very easy)
  - **Shock loading:** Mon/Wed/Fri — sprints, high-intensity plyos, high-variety plyo volume
- **Accessory work:** Returns to *general* (concentric, slow — e.g. regular RDLs and calf raises); keeps nervous system fresh and reduces injury risk
- Highest plyo intensity/variety of the entire macrocycle

### Post-Macrocycle Taper
- Reduce **volume by ~50%**, increase intensities
- Max effort jumping more frequently
- Squats: 5-4-3-2-1 protocols
- Power clean: daily velocity maxing
- General work volume low but maintained

### Jump Session Progression (40-Minute Window)
| Mesocycle | Warm-up (Low Effort) | Max Effort |
|---|---|---|
| Month 1 | 35 min | 5 min |
| Month 2 | 30 min | 10 min |
| Month 3 | 25 min | 15 min |
| Month 4 | 20 min | 20 min ← cap here |
- Do **not** increase max-effort jumping beyond the 20/20 split — leads to flatness and degraded training sessions

## Actionable Advice

- **Start with general work** specific to your jump type (one-foot vs. two-foot) before moving to specific work
- **Build a deload into Week 4** of every mesocycle — non-negotiable
- Use **clean pulls at 105–110% of your power clean max** in the strength and power phases to accelerate power clean progress
- Use **tempo runs** (30–45 sec, 3 min rest) in early mesocycles to improve body composition and cardiorespiratory fitness without excessive fatigue
- Track bar speed — when moving into Month 5, use a **stopwatch on squat sets** to enforce velocity intent
- Keep plyo volume and jump circuits **off the table** on general days once specific sessions intensify (Month 5+)
- Watch Boo Schexnayder's content on jump variety and plyo programming

- **Too much specificity too soon** — movements that mimic jumping too closely before the body is prepared leads to injury (back, knee, Achilles)
