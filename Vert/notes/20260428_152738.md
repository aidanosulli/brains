# Blueprint to a 40-Inch Vertical Leap
*John Evans — THP Strength*

---

## Core Principles

- **Fatigue-Fitness Model**: Performance is a function of fatigue vs. fitness. High training load builds fitness but also fatigue — you won't peak until fatigue drops. Macrocycles exploit this relationship.
- **Progressive Overload**: Each mesocycle builds on the last through stacking adaptations — never random, always potentiating the next phase.
- **Specificity Gradient**: General work early, specific work late. If training mimics jumping too closely too early, injury follows.
- **Variety + Individuality**: One-foot and two-foot jumpers require different exercise selections. No two cycles should be identical.
- **Balance Specific & General Days**: As specific days become more intense, general days must become proportionally easier — never push both simultaneously.

---

## Key Frameworks & Progressions

### Macrocycle Structure
- **Duration**: 6 months (macrocycle) containing **6 mesocycles** (~1 month each)
- **Week 4 of each mesocycle**: Built-in deload week
- **Training frequency**: 3 lifting days/week (e.g. Mon/Wed/Fri) + 2 general days (Tue/Thu)

---

### The 6 Mesocycles

#### Month 1 — Volume Building (General Preparatory Phase)
- Reps: **8–12**, Sets: **2–5**, Intensity: **65–70%**
- Exercise selection: *General work* (non-specific to jumping)
  - Two-foot jumpers → sumo deadlift, unilateral work
  - One-foot jumpers → deep squats, full range of motion
- Power exercises: **5 sets × 4 reps**
- Squats: **4–5 sets × 6–12 reps**
- Accessory work: **8–14 reps**
- General days: variety movements, build overall physical capacity

#### Month 2 — Intensification (Increased Specificity Introduction)
- Move toward more specific work (especially two-foot jumpers)
- Clean pulls / power cleans: **6 sets × 3–5 reps**
  - Clean pulls: ~**90%**
  - Power cleans: **75–85%**
- Rep ranges decrease slightly (e.g. 4 reps → 3 reps per set); sets increase to **5–7**
- Main lifts: **6–8 rep range**
- Accessory work shifts toward specificity to prime Month 3
  - RDLs, calf raises: **4–6 rep range**, intensities up to **~85%**
- General days: bodybuilding circuits (lower + upper body) + **tempo runs** (30–45 sec efforts, ~3 min rest — aerobic, not lactic)

#### Month 3 — Absolute Strength
- Back squat: **10 sets × 2–3 reps**
- Clean pulls: **105–110% of power clean 1RM** (builds first pull strength, makes power cleans feel easier)
- Accessory work transitions toward **eccentric strength** to prime Month 4:
  - Nordic hamstring curls
  - Two-up/one-down calf raises
- General days: **jump circuits in sand** (20–30 sec, varied jumps — toe touch, tuck jumps, single-leg hops); tempo runs increase in volume

#### Month 4 — Strength-Speed & Eccentric Loading
- Power clean: push toward **absolute maxes** (twice/week); clean pull on third day
- **Accentuated eccentrics** introduced:
  - Belt squat eccentric back squats: **110–115% of 1RM**
  - Eccentric split squats (supramaximal load, assisted concentric): 3 build-up sets + 3 working sets × 2–3 reps
- Partial ROM work introduced, matched to jump type:
  - One-foot → block pulls
  - Two-foot → full ROM floor pulls
- Accessory work: **elastic/high-velocity qualities** introduced
  - High-velocity calf raises, drop RDLs, bouncing calf raises
  - **~5–6 reps × ~2 sets** (keep volume low to avoid neural fatigue)
- General days: return to bodyweight circuits, remove tempo runs and jump circuits (protect tendons/joints)

#### Month 5 — Speed-Strength & Bar Velocity
- Shift from strength-speed → **speed-strength**
- Power snatch: ~**50% of power clean** weight
- Power clean: ~**60%** (focus on bar speed, not load)
- Hang power cleans introduced to load stretch-shortening cycle
- Squats: blend max strength + high-velocity work
  - Primer: **5-4-3 ascending** up to ~85%
  - High-velocity squat: 65–70% for timed sets of 5
- Accessory work primes Month 6 (shock loading):
  - Med ball throws (overhead, between legs, forward)
  - Low plyo volumes: box step-offs, step-up jumps
- General days: **fully restorative** — static flexibility, bodyweight circuits, core work, barefoot 100m walks/jogs; zero jump circuits

#### Month 6 — Maximum Specificity (Peaking)
- Plyo volume and intensity at **all-time high**
- Heavy sprint work integrated
- Lifting shifts to "feel good" maintenance:
  - Slow squats, slow calf raises, RDLs — very low intensity
  - Load management example: jump Mon/Thu, lift Tue/Fri (easy)
- Accessory work becomes **maximally general** (concentric-only, slow):
  - Regular RDLs, regular calf raises (slow) — keeps nervous system fresh

---

### Taper (Post-Macrocycle)
- Decrease volume by **~50%**
- Increase intensity: max effort jumps more frequently
- Squat protocol: **5-4-3-2-1**
- Power clean: daily velocity maxing
- Maintain general work at low volume

---

### Jump Session Progression (40-Minute Window)
| Mesocycle | Warm-Up Jumping | Max Effort Jumping |
|---|---|---|
| Month 1 | 35 min | 5 min |
| Month 2 | 30 min | 10 min |
| Month 3 | 25 min | 15 min |
| Month 4 | 20 min | 20 min ← **cap here** |

- Never exceed the 20/20 split — excessive max effort jumping causes flatness and degrades training quality

## Actionable Advice

- **Structure training in 6-month macrocycles** with a deload in week 4 of every mesocycle
- **Match exercise specificity to jump type**: two-foot jumpers use Olympic lift variations and hip-dominant work; one-foot jumpers use deep squats and full ROM movements
- **Use clean pulls at 105–110% of power clean** during the absolute strength phase to build pulling strength and make power cleans feel easier
- **Use accentuated eccentrics** (110–115% of 1RM) in Month 4 to maximally develop eccentric strength — a key determinant of jump height
- **Watch Boo Schexnayder's content** on plyo variety and programming
- **Keep accessory work always one step ahead** — use it to prime the *next* mesocycle's main lift demands
- **Time high-velocity squat sets** with a stopwatch to ensure true speed-strength intent

---

## Things to Avoid

- **Progressing too fast into specificity** — movements that closely mimic jumping too early in the macrocycle significantly increase injury risk
- **Keeping general days intense when specific days are intense** — these must inversely track each other
- **Exceeding 20 minutes of max effort jumping** per session — causes neural flatness and kills training quality
- **Exceeding ~2 sets of elastic/high-velocity accessory work** — neural fatigue accumulates quickly beyond this volume
- **Trying to combine max strength squats and high-velocity squats at equal intensity** in the same session — fries the nervous system and causes athletes to go flat
- **Ignoring the deload week** — it is structural, not optional
