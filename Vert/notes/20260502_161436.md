# Stripe Revenue Modeling: Invoices, Subscriptions & Daily MRR

## Core Principles

- **MRR = Monthly Recurring Revenue** — measures predictable, recurring SaaS income
- MRR is derived from **paid or open subscription invoices only**; invoice amounts are allocated evenly across their coverage period and summed into **daily MRR**, which rolls up to monthly/yearly figures
- Invoice intervals (reconstructed from created date + classification logic) and subscription intervals (from Stripe's `period_start`/`period_end`) are **not technically the same thing** — misalignment causes reporting, accounting, and customer experience problems
- Refunds are treated as **negative revenue** from the refund date through the end of the invoice interval
- Prorations are **monthly additions** layered on top of longer-interval invoices

---

## Key Frameworks & Progressions

### MRR Calculation Pipeline
```
Stripe Invoice
→ Extract: Product | Interval | Created Date
→ Compute Coverage Period (created_date + interval length)
→ Explode into 1 row per calendar day
→ Assign Daily MRR = amount / duration in days
→ Roll up to Monthly / Yearly MRR
```

### MRR Examples
| Scenario | Invoice | Amount | Result |
|---|---|---|---|
| Monthly | `in_1234` — Pro Monthly | $14.99 | MRR = $14.99/mo |
| Yearly | `in_5678` — Teams Yearly | $1,200 | MRR = $1,200 / 12 = **$100/mo** |

### Invoice Classification — 4 Required Dimensions
1. **Status** — must be `paid` or `open`
2. **Amount** — amount due minus any refunds (with refund initiation date)
3. **Product type** — determined by `product_id`; categories: Pro, Pro Plus, Teams, Proration, B2B Hardware, Shipping, Miscellaneous Fee, Unknown
4. **Interval** — determined by description text, product name, metadata, or date math:

| Interval | Detection Logic |
|---|---|
| **Monthly** | Description contains "monthly" (not "year"); or product name contains "month" |
| **Yearly** | Description contains "annual renewal" or "year" (not "monthly"/"prorate"); product name contains "yearly"; or date span = 12 months |
| **Quarterly** | Description says "quarterly"; or date span = 3 months |
| **6 Month** | Description says "6 month"; or date span = 6 months |
| **Multi-Year (2–5yr)** | Metadata `MultiYearSubYears` = 2/3/4/5; or date span ≈ 24/36/48/60 months; or description says "X years" |
| **Prorate** | Description contains "prorate" |
| **Charge** | One-time payment; stored separately |
| **Hardware/Shipping/Fee** | Keywords: devices/custom/card/crd/rec → Hardware; shipping/expedited/address → Shipping; fee/logo removal → Misc Fee |

### Invoice vs. Subscription Interval Sync States
- ✅ **Aligned** — invoice created date matches subscription `period_start` exactly
- ⚠️ **Invoice leads subscription** — invoice generated before service starts; customer paid but no service yet; future renewals invoice early, causing confusion
- ⚠️ **Invoice lags subscription** — service provided before payment confirmed; risk of free access period; can cause duplicate invoices if AEs request early renewal invoices

## Actionable Advice

### Every Invoice Must Have
- **Description** explicitly stating product type and interval (e.g., "Teams — Yearly Renewal") — primary fallback when metadata is absent
- **Accurate `created_date`** — anchors the invoice interval calculation
- **Correct `period_start` and `period_end`** — if billing period differs from creation date, edit supply dates explicitly (e.g., invoice created July 10 but covers May 1–Jun 1)
- **Complete billing address** including zip code and country
- **Proration metadata**: include `Months` field indicating coverage (e.g., 3-month proration at $45 → `Months: 3` → MRR = $15)
- **Multi-year metadata**: include `MultiYearSubYears = N` (e.g., `3` for a 3-year contract)

### Every Subscription Must Have
- **Product type / plan ID** (Pro, Teams, etc.)
- **Billing interval** reflected in Stripe settings
- **Accurate `period_start` and `period_end`**
- **`MultiYearSubYears` metadata** if applicable
- **Clear pause/resume** via Stripe's `pause_collection` — enables accurate future MRR modelling
- **Complete billing address on the Stripe Customer object**

---

## Things to Avoid

- Creating invoices **without descriptions** — fallback classification fails and interval becomes unknown
- **Misaligned invoice and subscription intervals** — causes customer confusion, duplicate invoices, and extra Accounting/AR work
- **Manual invoices outside normal Stripe flow** — hard to classify automatically
- **Invoices missing a complete billing address** (no zip/country)
- **Pausing/resuming subscriptions without matching invoice adjustments**
- **Multi-year contracts without `MultiYearSubYears` metadata** or with irregular start/end dates — interval detection degrades to heuristics
- **Processing refunds months later** without accounting for the gap — creates retroactive negative revenue distortions
- AEs requesting **early renewal invoices** without coordination — causes duplicate invoices in Stripe

---

## Key Takeaways

- **Daily MRR is the atomic unit**: all invoices are spread evenly across their coverage days, enabling precise monthly rollups regardless of billing interval
- **Invoice classification depends on description text, product metadata, and date math** — clean, consistent invoice descriptions are critical infrastructure, not just record-keeping
- **Invoice interval ≠ subscription interval**: they are reconstructed differently and can drift; keeping them aligned is essential for accurate reporting, customer experience, and AR efficiency
- **Multi-year and proration invoices require explicit metadata** (`MultiYearSubYears`, `Months`) — without them, MRR calculations will be incorrect
- **Refunds and prorations are modelled as adjustments in time**, not simple subtractions — refund date and proration period both matter for accurate daily MRR
