# Off-Season Strength & Power Session (Plyometric Box Protocol)

## Core Principles

- Off-season is the time to build strength and power capacity while managing overall load
- When running volumes are high, use **plyo boxes** to reduce landing stress without sacrificing explosive training quality
- Combines maximal strength (pressing, squatting) with reactive/elastic power work in the same session

## Key Frameworks & Progressions

**Upper Body Block**
- **Lock Lat Pull** — 2 × 12 (activation/warm-up)
- **Bench Press from Pins (Cluster Sets)** — 9 × 3, structured as 3 clusters of 3 reps with 20s intra-cluster rest
  - Pins remove the stretch-shortening cycle, emphasising pure contractile strength
- **Incline DB Prone Row** — 3 × failure (performed during bench rest periods — opportunistic volume)

**Lower Body / Power Block**

*Superset 1 (x2 rounds)*
- Knee ISO Push — 3 sec hold × 4 reps
- Reactive Drop Jumps w/ Single-Leg Catch — 30 sec

*Superset 2 (x2 rounds)*
- Single Leg RDL — 8 reps
- Seated Single Leg Broad Jump to Box — 4 reps

*Wave-Load Superset (Strength → Power → Expression)*
- Safety Bar Box Squat w/ Chains — 5 / 4 / 3 / 2 reps (descending wave)
- Seated Broad Jump to Box — 3 / 3 / 3 / 3 reps
- BW Countermovement Jump — 1 / 1 / 1 / 1 rep
  - Classic **PAP (Post-Activation Potentiation)** structure: heavy loaded → plyometric → free expression

**Accessory / Finish**
- Hip Flexor Cable Pull-In (Two-Leg) — 2 × 20

## Actionable Advice

- **Pair cluster sets with bench press from pins** to accumulate high-quality strength volume with reduced fatigue per rep
- **Use plyo boxes for jump landings** during high-run-load training blocks to protect joints while maintaining explosive output
- **Superset posterior chain strength (SL RDL) with seated broad jumps** — limits stretch-shortening reliance and targets raw power
- **Sequence box squats → broad jumps → CMJ** within the same block to potentiate and then express power freely
- Fill rest periods productively — e.g., prone rows during bench press rest intervals

## Things to Avoid

- Ignoring cumulative landing stress during periods of heavy running load — high-impact landings without modification risk overuse injury
- Skipping the iso/reactive pairing — the knee ISO push primes the quadriceps before reactive work; removing it reduces potentiation benefit
- Treating off-season power work as low-priority — this block demonstrates that quality explosive training can coexist with conditioning load if managed correctly

## Key Takeaways

- **Load management doesn't mean less intensity** — modifying landing surfaces (box targets) allows full-quality power work even during high running phases
- **Cluster sets with pins** are an effective off-season tool for accumulating strength volume with better bar speed and reduced fatigue
- **PAP sequencing** (heavy squat → seated broad jump → free CMJ) is a structured method to maximise power expression within a single block
- **Supersets and rest-period fillers** (prone rows during bench rest) increase session density without adding meaningful fatigue
- Single-leg variations (SL RDL, SL broad jump) build unilateral power and stability critical for sport performance
