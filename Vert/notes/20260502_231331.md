# Blueprint to a 40-Inch Vertical Leap
*John Evans — THP Strength*

---

## Core Principles

- **Fatigue-Fitness Model:** Performance is a function of fitness minus fatigue. High training loads build fitness but also accumulate fatigue — you won't always perform well during hard blocks, and that's intentional.
- **Progressive Overload:** Every cycle builds on the last; adaptations stack sequentially (potentiation).
- **Specificity Spectrum:** General work (less similar to jumping) builds the foundation; specific work (mimics jumping closely) peaks performance. These must be balanced — when specific work increases, general work must decrease, and vice versa.
- **Variety & Individuality:** One-foot and two-foot jumpers have different movement patterns and require different exercise selections.
- **Rest & Recovery are non-negotiable:** Injuries (back, knee, Achilles) result from excessive specificity or intensity without adequate recovery.
- **Macrocycle Structure:** 6-month training blocks allow stacking adaptations across mesocycles to maximise genetic potential.

---

## Key Frameworks & Progressions

### Macrocycle Overview
- **Duration:** 6 months (~1 mesocycle per month)
- **Structure:** 6 mesocycles inside 1 macrocycle, each priming the next
- **Training frequency:** 3 lifting days/week (e.g., Mon/Wed/Fri) + 2 general days (Tue/Thu)

---

### The 6 Mesocycles

#### Month 1 — Volume Building (General Preparatory Phase)
- **Focus:** Build base volume with general, non-specific work
- **Rep ranges:** 8–12 reps; 2–5 sets; ~65–70% intensity
- **Power work:** ~4 reps × 5 sets
- **Squat work:** 4–5 sets × 6–12 reps
- **Accessory:** 8–14 reps
- **Exercise selection by jump type:**
  - Two-foot jumpers → sumo deadlift, unilateral work
  - One-foot jumpers → deep squats, full ROM movements
- **General days:** High movement variety — goal is to build an all-around athletic base
- **Deload:** Built into week 4

#### Month 2 — Intensification (Transition to Specific Work)
- **Focus:** Increase intensity, reduce reps, introduce more specific exercises
- **Power work:** Clean pulls and power cleans introduced
  - Clean pulls: ~6 sets × 3–5 reps @ ~90%
  - Power cleans: 75–85%
- **Reps drop** (e.g., from 4 to 3 per set); sets increase to 5–7
- **Squats:** Move into 6–8 rep range
- **Accessory:** Start incorporating specific work (e.g., RDLs, calf raises at 4–6 reps, up to 85% intensity) — primes Month 3
- **General days:** Bodybuilding circuits (lower + upper body) + tempo running (30–45 sec runs, ~3 min rest — sub-maximal effort)

#### Month 3 — Absolute Strength
- **Focus:** Max strength development; heavy, low-rep work
- **Squats:** 10 sets × 2–3 reps (back squat)
- **Clean pulls:** 105–110% of power clean 1RM — builds force application in first and second pull
- **Accessory:** Introduce **eccentric strength** as primer for Month 4
  - Nordic hamstring curls
  - Two-up/one-down calf raises
- **General days:** Jump circuits in sand (20–30 sec, variety of jumps: toe touch, tuck, single-leg spin hops) + increased tempo work volume

#### Month 4 — Strength-Speed / Eccentric Strength
- **Focus:** Move bars fast; maximise power clean; build eccentric strength
- **Power clean:** Twice/week at near-maximal loads; clean pull on third day
- **Eccentric overload methods:**
  - **Accentuated eccentrics on belt squat:** Partner-assisted concentric; lower with supramaximal load (~110–115% of back squat 1RM)
  - **Eccentric split squats (safety bar):** 3 build-up sets normal + 3 working sets × 2–3 reps supramaximal eccentric
- **Partial ROM work:** Introduced based on jump type
  - One-foot jumpers: block pulls
  - Two-foot jumpers: full ROM from floor
- **Accessory:** High-velocity elastic work (bouncing calf raises, drop RDLs); ~5–6 reps × ~2 sets — keep volume low to avoid neural fatigue
- **General days:** Return to general — bodyweight circuits, reduce tempo, remove jump circuits; focus on recovery

#### Month 5 — Speed-Strength / Bar Velocity
- **Focus:** Shift from strength-speed → speed-speed; move bars as fast as possible
- **Olympic lifts:**
  - Power cleans: ~60% 1RM
  - Power snatch: ~50% of power clean 1RM
  - Hang power cleans introduced — loads stretch-shortening cycle, primes Month 6
- **Squats:** Blend max strength primer (5-4-3 ascending to ~85%) + high-velocity squats (65–70%, timed for speed)
- **Accessory / Primer for Month 6:** Med ball throws, shot put variations (overhead, between legs, forward); low-volume plyometrics (step-off box, step-up jumps); high variety
- **General days:** Near-complete rest/regeneration — static flexibility, core work, bodyweight circuits; no leg loading; barefoot 100m × 10 walk-back runs acceptable

#### Month 6 — Maximum Specificity
- **Focus:** Highest specificity and plyo volume of the year
- **Options:**
  - *Load management setup:* Jump Mon/Thu; feel-good lifts Tue/Fri (slow squats, slow calf raises, RDLs — very easy)
  - *Shock loading setup:* Heavy sprint and plyo work Mon/Wed/Fri
- **Plyometrics:** Highest intensity and variety of the macrocycle (volume stays relatively controlled)
- **Sprints:** Incorporated heavily
- **Accessory:** Becomes **more general** to protect the nervous system — slow, concentric-focused RDLs and calf raises

---

### Taper (Post-Macrocycle)
- Decrease total volume by ~50%
- Increase intensities and max effort jumping frequency
- Squat protocol: 5-4-3-2-1
- Power clean: daily velocity maxing
- Maintain general work at low volume

---

### Jump Session Progression (40-Minute Total Window)
| Mesocycle | Low-Effort Warm-Up | Max Effort Jumping |
|-----------|-------------------|-------------------|
| Month 1 | 35 min | 5 min |
| Month 2 | 30 min | 10 min |
| Month 3 | 25 min | 15 min |
| Month 4 | 20 min | 20 min *(cap here)* |

- Do **not** push max-effort jumping beyond the 20/20 split — leads to flatness and degraded sessions

## Actionable Advice

- **Start general, finish specific:** Use the first 1–2 months to build a broad athletic base before introducing jump-specific movements.
- **Match exercise selection to jump type:**
  - Two-foot jumpers → sumo deadlifts, unilateral work, full ROM pulls from floor
  - One-foot jumpers → deep squats, full ROM, block pulls
- **Use clean pulls at 105–110% of power clean** in Month 3 to strengthen first/second pull and make power cleans feel lighter in Month 4.
- **Use accentuated eccentrics** (110–115% of squat 1RM) to develop eccentric strength — a primary determinant of jumping ability.
- **Cap accessory elastic work** at ~5–6 reps × 2 sets to prevent neural fatigue accumulation.
- **Use tempo running** (sub-maximal, 30–45 sec runs, 3 min rest) to improve body composition and cardiorespiratory fitness without excessive fatigue.
- **Build in a deload every 4th week
