# Off-Season Strength & Power Session (Plyo Box Protocol)

## Core Principles

- During off-season periods with **high running loads**, reduce landing stress by using **plyo boxes** for jump landings — preserving explosive training quality without accumulating excessive impact
- Prioritise **reactive and ballistic power** work alongside traditional strength lifts
- Structure sessions to pair complementary movements in **supersets** for efficiency and contrast training effect

---

## Key Frameworks & Progressions

### Upper Body Block
- **Lock Lat Pull** — 2 × 12 (activation/warm-up quality)
- **Bench Press from Pins (Cluster Sets)** — 9 × 3, structured as **3 clusters of 3 reps with 20s intra-cluster rest**
  - Pins remove the stretch-shortening cycle, emphasising pure force production from a dead stop
- **Incline DB Prone Row** — 3 × failure, performed **during rest periods** of bench press sets (time-efficient filler)

---

### Lower Body / Power Block

**Superset 1** (×2 rounds)
- Knee ISO Push — 3 sec hold × 4 reps
- Reactive Drop Jumps with Single-Leg Catch — 30 sec

**Superset 2** (×2 rounds)
- Single Leg RDL — 8 reps
- Seated Single Leg Broad Jump to Box — 4 reps

**Wave-Load Superset — Descending Squat / Ascending Jump Contrast**
| Safety Bar Box Squat w/ Chains | Seated Broad Jump to Box | BW Countermovement Jump |
|---|---|---|
| 5 reps | 3 reps | 1 rep |
| 4 reps | 3 reps | 1 rep |
| 3 reps | 3 reps | 1 rep |
| 2 reps | 3 reps | 1 rep |

- Chains add **accommodating resistance** (heavier at lockout)
- Descending rep scheme on squat paired with stable jump volume = **potentiation contrast** effect

---

### Accessory Finish
- **Hip Flexor Cable Pull-In (Two-Leg)** — 2 × 20 reps

---

## Actionable Advice

- Use **box landings** (rather than floor) during high-volume running weeks to reduce cumulative eccentric landing stress while maintaining power output
- Programme **cluster sets** (e.g. 9 × 3 with 20s rest) for maximal strength work when heavy loads are the goal — allows more quality reps at high intensity than straight sets
- Fill strength rest periods with **low-fatigue accessory rows** to increase session density without compromising primary lifts
- Use **ISO holds before reactive work** (Knee ISO → Drop Jumps) to prime the nervous system
- Apply **contrast/post-activation potentiation (PAP)** by pairing heavy compound lifts with ballistic jumps in the same block

---

## Things to Avoid

- Ignoring cumulative landing load during off-season when running volume is already elevated — high-impact floor landings on top of running volume increases injury risk
- Skipping the **rest structure** in cluster sets — the 20s intra-cluster rest is what makes the method effective; collapsing it turns it into a standard set

---

## Key Takeaways

- **Load management is contextual** — modify exercise variation (e.g. box vs. floor jumps) based on total weekly stress, not just session load
- **Cluster sets** are a high-yield tool for building maximal strength while managing fatigue within a set
- **Contrast training** (heavy lift → ballistic jump) within the same session drives acute potentiation and trains the full force-velocity curve
- **Time efficiency** can be built into sessions by pairing accessory work into main lift rest periods without compromising quality
- Single-leg power work (SL RDL + SL Broad Jump to Box) is a key off-season priority for **asymmetry correction and sport-specific force application**
