# Blueprint to a 40-Inch Vertical Leap
*John Evans — THP Strength*

---

## Core Principles

- **Progressive overload** is non-negotiable — the program must systematically increase stress over time
- **Fatigue-Fitness Model**: performance = fitness minus fatigue; high training loads build fitness but also accumulate fatigue, so peaking requires strategic management of both
- **Specificity spectrum**: as specific work increases in intensity, general work must become *more* general to compensate — never max out both simultaneously
- **Sequential potentiation**: each mesocycle primes the next; accessory work in cycle N functions as a lead-in to the main focus of cycle N+1
- **Variety + individuality**: no two cycles should be identical; training must account for athlete type (one-foot vs. two-foot jumper)
- **Rest and recovery are training inputs**, not optional extras

---

## Key Frameworks & Progressions

### Macrocycle Structure
- **Duration**: 6 months (inspired by track & field periodisation)
- **Contains**: 6 mesocycles (~1 month each), each with a deload built into week 4
- **Arc**: General → Specific, Volume → Intensity, Slow → Fast

---

### The 6 Mesocycles

#### Month 1 — Volume Building (General Preparatory)
- **Focus**: Build base volume with general movements
- **Sets/Reps**: 2–5 sets × 8–12 reps @ 65–70% intensity
- **Power work**: 5 sets × 4 reps
- **Accessory**: 8–12 reps (up to 14 for fit athletes)
- **Movement selection**:
  - *Two-foot jumpers*: sumo deadlift, unilateral work
  - *One-foot jumpers*: deep squats, full range of motion
- **Frequency**: 3 lifting days/week (e.g. Mon/Wed/Fri) + general days (Tue/Thu) with varied movement circuits

#### Month 2 — Intensification (Specific Work Introduced)
- **Focus**: Increase intensity; shift toward more specific movements
- **Power work**: 5–7 sets × 3–4 reps
  - Clean pulls / power cleans: ~90% (clean pulls) / 75–85% (power cleans)
- **Main lifts**: Move to 6–8 rep range
- **Accessory**: Begin moving general accessory (e.g. RDLs, calf raises) to 4–6 reps @ up to 85% — *primes Month 3*
- **General days**: Bodybuilding circuits (lower + upper body) + tempo runs (30–45 sec efforts, ~3 min rest — aerobic, not lactic)

#### Month 3 — Absolute Strength
- **Focus**: Max strength; high load, low rep
- **Back squat**: 10 sets × 2–3 reps
- **Clean pulls**: 105–110% of power clean max (develops first and second pull strength)
- **Accessory**: Introduce **eccentric strength** work — primes Month 4
  - Nordic hamstring curls
  - Two-up/one-down calf raises
- **General days**: Jump circuits in sand (20–30 sec, varied jumps: toe touch, tuck, single-leg spin hops) + harder tempo work

#### Month 4 — Eccentric Strength & Power Development
- **Focus**: Move bars fast; build eccentric strength; increase triple-extension specificity
- **Power clean**: Hit absolute maxes; power clean 2×/week + clean pull 1×/week
- **Accentuated eccentrics**:
  - Belt squat: loaded eccentric @ 110–115% of max back squat (partner-assisted concentric)
  - Eccentric split squats: 3 build-up sets + 3 working sets × 2–3 reps (safety bar, rack-assisted concentric)
- **Partial ROM**: Introduced based on jump type
  - One-foot jumper → pulls from blocks
  - Two-foot jumper → pulls from floor, full ROM
- **Accessory**: High-velocity elastic work — bouncing calf raises, drop RDLs (5–6 reps × ~2 sets max)
- **General days**: Return to bodyweight/bodybuilding circuits; remove tempo runs and jump circuits to protect tendons and joints

#### Month 5 — Bar Speed / Speed-Strength
- **Focus**: Convert strength into speed; move from strength-speed → speed-strength
- **Olympic lifts**:
  - Power clean: ~60% for speed
  - Power snatch: ~50% of power clean max
  - Introduce hang power cleans (loads stretch-shortening cycle — primes Month 6)
- **Squats**: Blend max strength + high-velocity work
  - Primer: 5-4-3 ascending to ~85%
  - High-velocity squat: 65–70% — timed sets of 5 for max speed
- **Accessory**: Introduce shock loading — med ball throws (overhead, back-between-legs, forward, shot put style); low-volume plyometrics (step-off box, step-up jumps)
- **General days**: Fully restorative — static flexibility, core work, bodyweight circuits, barefoot 100m walk-back runs; **no jumping at all**

#### Month 6 — Peak Specificity
- **Focus**: Highest specificity and intensity; convert all adaptations into jumping performance
- **Options**:
  - *Load management*: Jump Mon/Thu; "feel good" lifts Tue/Fri (slow squats, slow calf raises, RDLs — very easy)
  - *Shock loading*: Heavy sprints + high-intensity plyometrics Mon/Wed/Fri
- **Plyos**: Highest intensity and variety of the year (volume kept relatively controlled)
- **Accessory**: Returns to *general/concentric* work — slow RDLs, slow calf raises; keeps nervous system fresh

---

### Post-Macrocycle Taper
- Decrease total volume ~50%
- Increase intensity: max-effort jumping more frequently
- Squat protocol: 5-4-3-2-1
- Power clean: daily velocity maxing
- Maintain general work at low volume

---

### Jump Session Progression (40-Minute Window)
Shift time allocation from warm-up to max-effort jumps across mesocycles:

| Mesocycle | Warm-Up Jumps | Max-Effort Jumps |
|-----------|--------------|-----------------|
| Month 1   | 35 min       | 5 min           |
| Month 2   | 30 min       | 10 min          |
| Month 3   | 25 min       | 15 min          |
| Month 4+  | 20 min       | 20 min (cap)    |

- Do **not** exceed 20 min of max-effort jumping — leads to flatness and degraded sessions

## Actionable Advice

- Train **3 days/week** for lifting (e.g. Mon/Wed/Fri) + 2 general days (Tue/Thu) — structure general days around what the specific days demand
- Always **build a deload into week 4** of each mesocycle
- Use **clean pulls at 105–110% of power clean** max to strengthen the pull and make power cleans feel easier in subsequent cycles
- Use **accentuated eccentrics** (110–115% of max) to develop eccentric strength — a major determinant of jump height
- Keep **accessory work as a primer** for the *next* cycle's main focus, not the current one
- Use **tempo runs** early in the macrocycle to improve body composition and cardiovascular fitness without excessive fatigue
- When specificity peaks, **make general days genuinely easy** — restorative only
- Reference: *Boo Schexnayder* on plyo variety (podcast recommended)

---

## Things to Avoid

- **Trying to jump higher every week** — performance fluctuates due to the fatigue-fitness relationship; trust the macrocycle
- **Exceeding 20 minutes of max-effort jumping** per session — causes flatness and poor performance
