# Standing vs Max Vertical Jump — Understanding the Key Distinctions

> **Source:** https://www.tiktok.com/@jumpsbychi/video/7564848380271283487
> **Date:** 2026-07-07

CONFIDENCE: medium

## Core Principles

- "Vertical jump" is an umbrella term — it simply means jumping for height (vertically)
- Multiple valid types of vertical jumps exist; neither is more "real" or legitimate than the other
- Different contexts and sports organisations measure different types depending on what they need to assess

## Key Frameworks & Progressions

**Types of Vertical Jumps:**
- **Standing vertical jump** — no approach or momentum; measured from a standstill
- **Max vertical / approach vertical jump** — uses a running or step approach to maximise height
- **Hands-on-hips jump** — arms held fixed to isolate leg power
- **Seated vertical jump** — eliminates leg drive from the approach entirely

**How Major Sports Orgs Measure:**
- **NFL Combine** — standing vertical jump only
- **NBA Draft Combine** — measures *both* standing and max vertical jump

## Things to Avoid

- Dismissing approach/run-up jumps as "cheating" or not counting as real vertical jumps
- Treating one metric as superior to the other — they measure related but distinct athletic qualities
- Conflating different jump types when comparing athletes across contexts

## Key Takeaways

- There is no single "vertical jump" — the term requires a qualifier (standing, max, etc.) to be meaningful
- A max vertical will typically exceed a standing vertical; this doesn't invalidate either metric
- Context determines which type matters — know what's being measured before making comparisons
- Comparing jump scores across types without acknowledging the difference produces misleading conclusions
