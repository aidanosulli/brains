# Six-Mesocycle Macrocycle Blueprint for Achieving a 40-Inch Vertical Leap

> **Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Icml891MLHA
> **Date:** 2026-06-01

CONFIDENCE: medium

## Core Principles

- **Fatigue-Fitness Model**: Performance is a function of fatigue and fitness simultaneously — high training load builds fitness but also accumulates fatigue, suppressing peak performance. Macrocycles exploit this by stacking adaptations sequentially
- **Progressive Overload**: Each mesocycle builds on the previous; volume, intensity, and specificity shift systematically rather than randomly
- **Specificity Balance**: As specific work increases in intensity, general work must become *more* general to balance neural/physical load — doing both simultaneously causes injury
- **Variety & Individuality**: Training must account for jump type (one-foot vs. two-foot) and individual capacity
- **Rest & Recovery**: Built-in deloads (week 4 of each month) and recovery days are non-negotiable

---

## Key Frameworks & Progressions

### Macrocycle Structure
- **Duration**: 6 months (one macrocycle)
- **Sub-structure**: 6 mesocycles (one per month), each ~3 weeks of work + 1 deload week
- **Training frequency**: 3 specific days/week (e.g., Mon/Wed/Fri) + 2 general days (Tue/Thu)
- **Taper**: After the full macrocycle, reduce volume ~50%, increase intensity; use 5-4-3-2-1 squat protocols and daily velocity maxing on power clean

---

### Mesocycle Breakdown

**Month 1 — Volume Building (General Preparatory)**
- Focus: High-rep, moderate-intensity general work
- Sets/reps: 2–5 sets × 8–12 reps; power work: 5 sets × 4 reps
- Intensity: 65–70%
- Exercise selection by jump type:
  - Two-foot jumpers → sumo deadlifts, unilateral work
  - One-foot jumpers → deep squats, full ROM movements
- Accessory: 8–14 reps depending on fitness level
- General days: Variety of movements, building broad athletic capacity

**Month 2 — Intensification (Increased Specificity)**
- Clean pulls & power cleans introduced more heavily
  - Clean pulls: 6 sets × 3–5 reps @ ~90%
  - Power cleans: 75–85%
- Main lifts: Reduce reps (e.g., 4 → 3); increase sets to 5–7, building week-over-week
- Main strength work: 6–8 rep range
- Accessory: Begin moving toward more specific work (e.g., RDLs/calf raises at 4–6 reps, up to 85% intensity) — primes Month 3
- General days: Bodybuilding circuits (lower + upper) + tempo running (improves body composition and cardiorespiratory fitness)

**Month 3 — Absolute Strength**
- Back squat: 10 sets × 2–3 reps
- Clean pulls: 105–110% of power clean max (builds first/second pull strength)
- Accessory: Eccentric strength work as primer for Month 4
  - Nordic hamstring curls, two-up/one-down calf raises
- General days: Jump circuits in sand (20–30 sec, varied jumps — toe touch, tuck, single-leg spins); harder tempo work (30–45 sec runs, 3-min rest, avoid lactic overload)

**Month 4 — Strength-Speed & Accentuated Eccentrics**
- Power clean: Twice/week, push toward absolute max; clean pull on third day
- Accentuated eccentric squats: 110–115% of back squat max (partner-assisted descent)
- Eccentric split squats: Supramaximal load with rack assistance, 3 working sets × 2–3 reps
- Partial ROM work introduced and calibrated by jump type:
  - One-foot jumpers → block pulls
  - Two-foot jumpers → full ROM from floor
- Accessory: High-velocity elastic work (bouncing calf raises, drop RDLs) — ~5–6 reps × ~2 sets max (avoid neural fatigue)
- General days: Return to general bodybuilding circuits; remove tempo runs and jump circuits to protect joints/tendons

**Month 5 — Speed-Strength & Bar Velocity**
- Shift from strength-speed → speed-speed:
  - Power clean: ~60% for speed
  - Power snatch: ~50% of power clean max
  - Hang power cleans introduced (develops stretch-shortening cycle in Olympic lifts)
- Squat: Blend max strength primer (5-4-3 up to 85%) with high-velocity squats (65–70%, timed for speed)
- Accessory: Shock loading primer for Month 6 — med ball throws (overhead, back, between legs); low plyo volumes (box step-offs, step-up jumps)
- General days: Fully general — static stretching, bodyweight circuits, core work, barefoot running (100m walk-back × 10); **no jumping on general days**

**Month 6 — Maximum Specificity & Peaking**
- Highest specificity and highest volume of specific work
- Options:
  - Load management split: Jump Mon/Thu; feel-good lifts Tue/Fri (slow squats, slow calf raises, RDLs — very low intensity)
  - Shock loading split: Mon/Wed/Fri with heavy sprint work and plyo volume (highest intensity/variety of plyos across the year)
- Accessory: Reverts to general/concentric-only work (regular RDLs, slow calf raises) — keeps nervous system fresh and reduces injury risk

### Jump Session Progression (40-Minute Total Window)
| Mesocycle | Low-Effort Warm-Up | Max Effort Jumping |
|---|---|---|
| Month 1 | 35 min | 5 min |
| Month 2 | 30 min | 10 min |
| Month 3 | 25 min | 15 min |
| Month 4 | 20 min | 20 min *(cap here)* |

- Exceeding the 20/20 split causes flatness and degrades performance in subsequent sessions

## Actionable Advice

- **Match general work to jump type**: Two-foot jumpers use sumo/unilateral early; one-foot jumpers use deep squats/full ROM
- **Build deload week 4 into every mesocycle** without exception
- **Use clean pulls at 105–110% of power clean** in Month 3 to prime the second pull and make power cleans easier in Month 4
- **Cap high-velocity accessory work** (elastic/bouncing movements) at ~2 sets × 5–6 reps to avoid neural fatigue buildup
- **Time high-velocity squat sets** with a stopwatch (5 reps at 65–70%) to enforce bar speed intent
- **Use tempo running** (30–45 sec, 3-min rest, sub-lactic) on general days in Months 2–3 to improve body composition and aerobic base without excessive fatigue
- **Implement sand jump circuits** in Month 3 general days (20–30 sec, varied movements) for low-impact plyo volume
- **Taper post-macrocycle**: Cut volume ~50%, increase intensities, max-effort jump more frequently

---

## Things to Avoid

- **Doing specific and general work at high intensity simultaneously** — primary cause of overuse injuries (back, knee, Achilles)
- **Increasing max-effort jumping beyond the 20-minute cap** in any session — leads to flatness and poor training quality
- **Excessive neural loading from combining high-intensity max strength squats with high-velocity squats** — will fry athletes; use one as a primer, not both at full load
- **Keeping general days intense as the program progresses** — general days must become *more general* as specific days become *more specific*
- **Ignoring individuality**: Program must account for jump type and current fitness level
- **Skipping deload weeks** — accumulation without recovery prevents the fitness-fatigue curve from expressing performance gains

## Key Takeaways

- A 6-month macrocycle with 6 sequentially potentiating mesocycles is the core structure — each month primes the next via accessory work and specificity
