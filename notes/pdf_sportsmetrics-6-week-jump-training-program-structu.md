# Sportsmetrics 6-Week Jump Training Program Structure and Exercises

> **Source:** sportsmetrics-jump-training-log.pdf
> **Date:** 2026-07-07

## Core Principles

- Jump training is periodised across 3 phases: Technique → Fundamentals → Performance
- Sessions run 3x per week (Mon/Wed/Fri) across 6 weeks
- Total ground contacts per session are capped and progressively increased to manage load
- Proper form takes priority over volume — stop when form breaks or fatigue sets in
- Rest intervals are double the jump duration; 30 seconds rest for rep-based jumps
- Soft-surface jumps (marked ♦) must be performed on a track or gym mats

---

## Key Frameworks & Progressions

### Phase Structure

| Phase | Weeks | Focus | Max Daily Contacts |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 – Technique Development | 1–2 | Form and basic patterns | 120 → 150 |
| 2 – Fundamentals | 3–4 | Increased duration/volume | 160 → 190 |
| 3 – Performance | 5–6 | Power, single-leg, complex movements | 220 → 250 |

### Session Structure
- **Warm-up:** 2 laps jogging/skipping + 2 laps side shuffle + 5–10 min stretching
- **Training block:** Phase-appropriate exercises (timed or rep-based)
- **Cool-down:** Walk or light jog + 5–10 min stretching + ice if needed (15–20 min)

### Exercise Progression by Phase

**Phase 1 (Weeks 1–2) — Technique:**
- Wall jumps, Tuck jumps, Broad jump-stick landing, Squat jumps
- Cone jumps (side-to-side, back-to-front), 180 jumps, Bounding in place
- Duration increases from 20 sec → 25 sec; broad jumps 5 → 10 reps

**Phase 2 (Weeks 3–4) — Fundamentals:**
- Wall jumps, Tuck jumps, Squat jumps, Cone jumps, Scissors jump
- New additions: Jump-jump-jump-vertical, Bounding for distance, Hop-hop-stick (double leg)
- Duration holds at 30 sec; bounding runs increase 1 → 2

**Phase 3 (Weeks 5–6) — Performance:**
- Wall jumps, Scissors jump, Squat jumps
- New additions: Step-jump up-down-vertical (box), Single-leg jumps for distance, Jump into bounding, Single-leg hop-hop-stick
- Jump-into-bounding runs increase 3 → 4; step-jump reps increase 5 → 10

## Actionable Advice

### Exercise Execution Cues
- **Wall Jumps (Ankle Bounces):** Knees slightly bent, arms raised overhead, bounce off toes — minimal knee flexion
- **Tuck Jumps:** Drive both knees to chest as high as possible; repeat quickly ♦
- **Broad Jump-Stick Landing:** Two-footed max distance jump; hold landing with knees bent for **5 seconds**
- **Squat Jumps (Frog Jumps):** Jump with arms overhead; land in squat touching both hands to floor ♦
- **180 Jumps:** Rotate 180° in mid-air, arms at 90° at sides; hold each landing **2 seconds**, alternate directions
- **Scissors Jump:** Stride position start; alternate feet in mid-air
- **Hop-Hop-Stick:** Three single-leg hops; hold third landing **5 seconds** with knees slightly bent; alternate legs ♦
- **Single-Leg Jumps for Distance:** One-legged hop for max distance; hold landing **2 seconds**, knee slightly bent ♦
- **Step-Jump Up-Down-Vertical:** Jump onto 6–8 inch box or stacked mats → jump forward off box → immediately jump straight up with arms overhead
- **Jump into Bounding:** Two-footed broad jump → land single leg → progress into bounding for distance ♦
- **Bounding for Distance:** Start in place, progressively increase stride distance, keep knees high
- **Cone Jumps:** Feet together; jump side-to-side, then forward/backward over cones ♦

### Load Management
- Track total contacts per session against the daily maximum goal
- Increase contact targets weekly as prescribed — do not exceed phase caps

---

## Things to Avoid

- Continuing exercise when form degrades or fatigue is evident — stop immediately
- Insufficient rest — never rest less than double the jump duration between sets
- Performing ♦-marked exercises on hard/unpadded surfaces
- Skipping the warm-up or cool-down protocol
- Rushing progression — each week has distinct volume targets that should not be skipped

---

## Key Takeaways

- A structured 6-week, 3-phase plyometric program that progressively increases complexity and ground contact volume from 120 to 250 contacts per session
- Phase 1 emphasises bilateral, controlled landings; Phase 2 introduces distance and combo jumps; Phase 3 adds single-leg and box-based power movements
- Landing hold times (2–5 seconds) are explicitly prescribed to develop deceleration control and joint stability
- Rest-to-work ratio of 2:1 is a non-negotiable structural rule throughout all phases
- Exercise surface matters — several high-impact single-leg movements require a track or mat to reduce injury risk
