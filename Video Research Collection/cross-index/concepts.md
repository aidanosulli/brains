# Key Concepts: Video Research Collection — 2026-05-25

## Progressive Training Structure

- **Fatigue-Fitness Model**: Performance equals fitness minus fatigue; training loads build fitness but also accumulate fatigue, requiring strategic sequencing to peak at the right time.
- **Progressive Overload with Variety**: Training must be continuously increased in difficulty while remaining individualized and varied so that no two cycles are identical.

## Performance Optimization Principles

- **Specificity of Adaptation**: The body adapts only as much as its environment demands, so training must be specific and varied to continuously drive progress.
- **Genetic Ceiling with Controllable Improvement**: While genetics set a performance ceiling, improvement within that ceiling is always within an athlete's control regardless of starting vertical or age.
- **Plateau Persistence**: Plateaus are normal parts of training and not indicators of failure; personal records often come years after apparent stagnation if training continues.

## Technical Skill Development

- **Two-Foot Jump Technical Practice**: Two-foot jumping is a distinct skill requiring dedicated technical drills to improve takeoff mechanics.
