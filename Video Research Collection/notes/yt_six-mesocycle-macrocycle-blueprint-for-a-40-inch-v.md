# Six-Mesocycle Macrocycle Blueprint for a 40-Inch Vertical Leap

> **Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Icml891MLHA
> **Date:** 2026-05-25

CONFIDENCE: medium

## Core Principles

- **Fatigue-Fitness Model**: Performance = fitness minus fatigue. High training loads build fitness but also fatigue — macrocycles exploit this by stacking adaptations sequentially so athletes peak at the right time
- **Progressive Overload with Variety**: Programs must be progressively overloaded, individualised, and varied — no two cycles should be identical
- **Specificity Balance**: As specific work increases in intensity, general work must become *more* general to compensate — running both high simultaneously causes injury
- **Potentiation Sequencing**: Each mesocycle's accessory work primes the *following* mesocycle's main lifts (e.g., eccentric work primes elastic/reactive work)
- **Macrocycle Length**: 6-month macrocycles with 6 mesocycles inside; modelled after track and field periodisation

---

## Key Frameworks & Progressions

### Macrocycle Structure (6 Mesocycles)

**Month 1 — Volume Building (General Preparatory)**
- Reps: 8–12; Sets: 2–5; Intensity: ~65–70%
- Deload in week 4
- Frequency: 3 lifting days/week (e.g., Mon/Wed/Fri) + general days (Tue/Thu)
- Power work: ~4 reps × 5 sets
- Squat: 4–5 sets × 6–12 reps
- Accessory: 8–14 reps depending on fitness
- **General work emphasis**: movements that *least* mimic jumping
  - Two-foot jumpers: sumo deadlift, unilateral work
  - One-foot jumpers: deep squats, full ROM movements

**Month 2 — Intensification (Moving Toward Specific Work)**
- Shift toward more specific movements
- Clean pulls & power cleans introduced: 6 sets × 3–5 reps @ ~90% (clean pulls), 75–85% (power cleans)
- Sets increase week-over-week (start low, build up)
- Main lifts: move to 6–8 rep range
- Accessory: general work moves to 4–6 reps @ ~85%; more specific selections
- General days: bodybuilding circuits + tempo running (improves body composition and cardiorespiratory fitness)

**Month 3 — Absolute Strength**
- Main lifts: 10×3 or 10×2 back squat
- Clean pulls: 105–110% of power clean max (builds first pull strength, makes power cleans feel lighter)
- Accessory: introduce **eccentric strength** as primer for next cycle
  - Nordic hamstring curls, two-up/one-down calf raises
- General days: jump circuits in sand (20–30 sec, varied jumps: toe touch, tuck jumps, single-leg spinning hops)
- Tempo work: 30–45 sec runs, ~3 min rest — keep effort sub-lactic

**Month 4 — Eccentric & Strength-Speed (Peak Power Development)**
- Power clean: 2×/week + clean pull on 3rd day; push toward absolute max
- Eccentric loading:
  - Accentuated eccentric back squat via belt squat: 110–115% of max
  - Eccentric split squats: supramaximal load, 3 build-up sets + 3 working sets × 2–3 reps
- Partial ROM introduced based on jump type (blocks for one-foot; floor pulls for two-foot)
- Accessory: elastic qualities — high-velocity calf raises, drop RDLs, bouncing calf raises (~5–6 reps × 2 sets; do not exceed)
- General days: return to bodybuilding circuits, body weight circuits; remove tempo runs and jump circuits to protect tendons/joints

**Month 5 — Bar Speed / Speed-Strength**
- Shift from strength-speed → speed-strength
- Lifts: power snatch (~50% of power clean max), hang power cleans, lighter power cleans (~60%)
- Squat: blend max strength + high-velocity work
  - Primer sets: 5-4-3 ramping to ~85%
  - High-velocity squats: ~65–70% for time (stopwatch 5-rep sets)
- Accessory: shock loading — med ball throws (overhead, between legs, forward); low-volume plyos (step-off box, step-up jumps)
- General days: fully regenerative — static flexibility, core, bodyweight circuits; **no leg loading**; barefoot 100m walk-backs (×10) as alternative

**Month 6 — Peak Specificity**
- Highest specificity and specific volume of the macrocycle
- Option A (Load Management): Jump Mon/Thu; "feel good" lifts Tue/Fri — slow squats, slow calf raises, light RDLs
- Option B: Shock loading Mon/Wed/Fri with heavy sprint and plyo emphasis
- Plyo volume remains relatively low across the year — if used heavily, it's *only* in month 6
- Accessory: reverts to **general/concentric** work (regular RDLs, slow calf raises) to keep nervous system fresh

### Taper (Post-Macrocycle)
- Decrease volume ~50%; increase intensity
- Max effort jumping more frequently
- Squat: 5-4-3-2-1 protocols
- Power clean: daily velocity maxing
- Maintain general work at low volume

### Jump Session Progression (40-Minute Window)
| Mesocycle | Low-Effort Warm-Up | Max-Effort Jumping |
|-----------|-------------------|-------------------|
| Month 1   | 35 min            | 5 min             |
| Month 2   | 30 min            | 10 min            |
| Month 3   | 25 min            | 15 min            |
| Month 4+  | 20 min (cap)      | 20 min (cap)      |

- Do **not** exceed 20 min of max-effort jumping — causes flatness and degrades training quality

## Actionable Advice

- **Structure lifting 3×/week** (Mon/Wed/Fri) with general/recovery days on Tue/Thu
- **Always deload in week 4** of each mesocycle
- **Start power work at lower set counts and add sets weekly** rather than jumping to full volume
- Use **clean pulls at 105–110% of power clean max** to build pulling strength without missing lifts
- Use **accentuated eccentrics** (belt squat, eccentric split squats) at 110–115% of max to develop eccentric strength — a primary determinant of jump height
- **Tempo runs** (30–45 sec, 3 min rest, sub-lactic effort) as a low-risk fitness builder on general days
- **Sand jump circuits** (20–30 sec, varied movements) are a good general day tool during month 3
- When specificity peaks, **make general days explicitly easier** — this is not optional
- Reference Boo Schexnayder's work for variety principles in plyometric programming

---

## Things to Avoid

- **Running high specificity and high intensity simultaneously on all days** — primary injury risk (back pain, knee pain, Achilles)
- **Increasing max-effort jumping beyond 20 minutes** — causes athletes to go flat
- **Exceeding ~5–6 reps × 2 sets on high-velocity accessory work** — neural fatigue accumulates rapidly
- **Trying to jump higher every week** — performance fluctuates by design; trust the fatigue-fitness model
- **Keeping tempo/jump circuits in during month 4–5 general days** — remove them when specific work peaks to protect joints and tendons
- **Over-specificity too early** — movements that mimic jumping too closely, introduced too soon, lead to injury

## Key Takeaways

- A 40-inch vertical requires a structured **6-month macrocycle** with sequentially potentiated mesocycles — not random training or short-term peaking
- Each cycle serves a specific role: **Volume → Intensification → Absolute Strength → Eccentric/Power → Bar Speed → Peak Specificity**, with each phase priming the next
- **Specificity and general work must be inversely balanced** throughout the year — as one rises, the other must fall
