# Pacific Crossing: Tahiti to Washington via Hawaii

## Key Frameworks & Progressions

**Route & Passage Structure**
- **Leg 1 – Morea (French Polynesia) → Hawaii**
  - Upwind sailing in 25 knots with solid swell — a wet, demanding start
  - Passed through the doldrums → crossed the equator → entered Northern Hemisphere
  - Duration: **16 days at sea**
  - Landfall: Molokai cliffs visible first, then Honolulu
- **Leg 2 – Hawaii → Pacific Northwest (Washington)**
  - Conditions progressively colder, greyer, foggier
  - Closest land at points: Alaska
  - Notable wildlife: frequent whale sightings
  - Duration: **~17 days** (33 days total minus 16)
- **Total passage time: 33 days at sea** (plus a pit stop in Hawaii)

**Preparation Phase**
- Several weeks of boat prep in Morea before departure

## Key Takeaways

- The Tahiti-to-US Pacific crossing is unusually demanding because it requires **sailing upwind for thousands of miles** before picking up westerly winds toward the coast
- Passage planning involves distinct phases: upwind slog → doldrums → trade winds → cold North Pacific
- The **80-foot classic yacht *Latifa*** demonstrated that delivery passages on large classic vessels require a seasoned crew and extended prep time
- Conditions shift dramatically across the passage — from tropical South Pacific to cold, fog-bound North Pacific near Alaskan latitudes
- Ocean crossings require mental and physical adjustment; finding "sea legs" typically takes roughly the **first week**
